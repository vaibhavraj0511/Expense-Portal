Dashboard

The Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of your financial health at a glance. It's your home base for understanding your overall financial position.

Net Worth: Total assets (accounts, investments) minus liabilities (loans, credit card debt). Gives you your overall financial position. Calculated as (Account Balances + Investments) - (Loans + Credit Card Debt).
Spend Rate: Average daily spending based on recent expenses. Helps you understand your spending patterns. Calculated from the last 30 days of expenses divided by 30.
Savings Rate: Percentage of income saved. Calculated as (Income - Expenses) / Income. A higher rate indicates better financial health.
Spending Heatmap: Visual calendar showing daily spending patterns. Darker colors indicate higher spending days. Helps identify high-spending days of the month.
Daily Spending Profile: Classifies each day into spending tiers (₹0, ₹1-₹99, ₹100-₹499, ₹500-₹999, ₹1k-₹4.9k, ₹5k+). This helps you understand your spending patterns throughout the month.
Spending Insights: Key observations about your spending habits, top categories, and trends. AI-generated insights based on your spending data.
Upcoming Bills Widget: Shows bills due in the next 7 days with urgency indicators (red for urgent, amber for soon, green for later).
Recent Transactions: Quick view of your latest 5 expenses and 5 income entries. Click to view full details or navigate to the respective sections.
Quick Add Buttons: Fast access to add expenses, income, or transfers without navigating to specific sections. Opens the respective modals directly.
AI Insights

Smart AI-powered analysis of your financial data. All analysis is done locally on your device - no API key or internet connection needed. The AI analyzes your spending patterns, income, and savings to provide actionable insights.

Financial Health Score: Overall score (0-100) based on your income, expenses, savings rate, and debt levels. Higher scores indicate better financial health. Factors include: savings rate (30%), debt-to-income ratio (25%), spending consistency (20%), income stability (15%), and emergency fund (10%).
Spending Forecasts: Predictions of future spending based on historical patterns. Uses your last 3-6 months of data to forecast next month's spending by category. Helps you plan your budget proactively.
Anomaly Detection: Alerts for unusual spending patterns that deviate from your norms. Identifies expenses that are significantly higher than your average for that category or time period. Helps catch fraud or unexpected spending.
Budget Optimization Tips: Suggestions for improving your budget allocation. Analyzes your spending vs budget and recommends adjustments to optimize your financial health.
Category Trends: Shows top 8 categories with 3-month growth or decline trends. Compares current month spending to the average of the previous 2 months. Categories with significant increases are flagged for attention.
Spending Increases: Shows top 3 categories with highest spending increase. Displays the percentage increase and absolute amount. Helps identify areas where spending is growing unexpectedly.
Well Done: Shows top 3 categories with highest spending decrease. Celebrates your success in reducing spending in these areas. Positive reinforcement for good financial habits.
Daily Spending Profile: AI-classified spending tiers for each day of the month. Uses machine learning to classify days into spending patterns (e.g., "high spending weekends" vs "low spending weekdays").
Savings Rate Analysis: Analysis of your savings rate over time. Shows trend of your savings rate month-over-month. Helps you track progress toward financial goals.
Income vs Expense Comparison: Comparison of your income and expense trends. Shows whether your expenses are growing faster than your income. Helps identify if you're living within your means.
Budget Health Analysis: Analyzes how well you're sticking to your budgets. Shows percentage of budgets that are on track, at risk, or exceeded. Provides recommendations for improvement.
Local AI: All analysis is done locally on your device using JavaScript. No data is sent to external servers. Your financial data stays private and secure.
Read-Only: All insights are read-only analysis of your data. The AI doesn't make any changes to your entries - it only analyzes and reports.
Expenses

Record and track all your outgoing payments and expenses. This is the core section for tracking where your money goes.

Expense Cards: Each expense displays amount, category, date, payment method, and notes. Cards are color-coded by category for quick visual identification. Shows budget progress bar if category has a budget.
Pagination: Shows 12 expenses per page for better performance. Use pagination controls (Previous/Next buttons or page numbers) to navigate through large lists.
Add Expense Modal: Form to add new expenses with fields for description, amount, category, date, payment method, account, and notes. Category dropdown is auto-populated from your categories.
Edit/Delete: Modify or remove existing expense entries. Changes update account balances automatically. Edit opens the modal with pre-filled data, delete requires confirmation.
Search & Filter: Search expenses by description or filter by category, date range, or payment method. Search is real-time and updates as you type.
Category Badges: Visual badges showing expense category for quick identification. Each category has its own color and icon.
Account Linking: When you link an expense to an account, the account balance is automatically debited. This ensures your account balances are always accurate.
Budget Progress: If the expense category has a budget for the current month, shows a progress bar indicating how much of the budget has been used. Turns red when over budget.
Integration: Expense data is used in Budgets, Tax Summary, AI Insights, and Analytics. Changes here reflect everywhere.
Income

Record and track all your incoming payments and income sources. This section helps you understand where your money comes from.

Income Cards: Each income entry displays source, amount, date, account, and notes. Cards show income source badges for quick identification.
Pagination: Shows 12 income entries per page for better performance and navigation.
Add Income Modal: Form to add new income with fields for source, amount, date, account, and notes. Source dropdown is auto-populated from your income sources.
Edit/Delete: Modify or remove income entries. Changes update account balances automatically. Edit opens modal with pre-filled data.
Search & Filter: Search income by source or filter by date range or account. Real-time search updates as you type.
Source Badges: Visual badges showing income source (salary, freelance, investment, etc.). Each source has its own color.
Account Linking: When you link income to an account, the account balance is automatically credited. Keeps your balances accurate.
Integration: Income data is used in Salary Splitter, AI Insights, Cash Flow, and Tax Summary. Changes reflect everywhere.
Transfers

Move money between your own accounts without affecting your net worth. Useful for consolidating funds or paying credit cards from bank accounts.

Transfer Cards: Each transfer shows from/to accounts, amount, date, and notes. Clearly shows the direction of money flow.
Pagination: Shows 12 transfers per page for easier navigation.
Add Transfer Modal: Form to add new transfers with from account, to account, amount, date, and notes. Account dropdowns show all your accounts.
Edit/Delete: Modify or remove transfer entries. Changes reverse account balance updates (debits/credits are reversed).
Search & Filter: Search transfers or filter by date range or accounts. Real-time search for easy lookup.
Auto-Debit/Credit: From account is automatically debited, to account is automatically credited. Ensures accurate balances.
Net Worth Impact: Transfers don't change your net worth since money is just moving between your accounts. Assets and liabilities remain unchanged.
Recurring

Automate recurring expenses and income that happen on a schedule. Great for subscriptions, rent, salary, and other regular transactions.

Recurring Cards: Each recurring entry shows name, type (expense/income), amount, frequency (weekly/monthly/yearly), next date, category, and account. Shows pause status if paused.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new recurring entries or modify existing ones. Full control over all parameters.
Pause/Resume: Temporarily stop recurring entries from being created without deleting them. Useful for temporary suspensions (e.g., paused subscription).
Auto-Creation: Every time the app loads, it checks for due recurring entries and creates them automatically. No manual entry needed for regular transactions.
Backdating: If the app wasn't opened for a while, it backdates missed recurring entries to fill gaps. Ensures no entries are missed.
Account Linking: When recurring entries are created, they automatically debit/credit the linked account. Keeps balances accurate.
Next Date Display: Shows when the next entry will be created. Helps you plan upcoming transactions.
Frequency Options: Weekly (every 7 days), Monthly (same day each month), Yearly (same date each year). Flexible scheduling options.
Budgets

Set monthly spending limits per category and track your budget health. Essential for controlling spending and achieving financial goals.

Budget Cards: Each budget shows category, monthly limit, amount spent, remaining amount, and a visual progress bar. Color-coded: green (under budget), amber (near limit), red (over budget).
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new budgets or modify existing ones. Set monthly limits per category.
Progress Bars: Visual indicators showing how much of the budget has been used. Shows percentage and absolute amounts.
Overspending Alerts: Categories that exceed their budget are highlighted in red for quick identification. Helps you spot problem areas immediately.
Auto-Roll Monthly: Budgets automatically roll over to the next month from your template. No need to recreate budgets each month.
Budget Health: Overall budget health is shown in Analytics with visual charts. Shows percentage of budgets on track vs exceeded.
Integration: Budgets pull expense data automatically from the Expenses section. Real-time updates as you add expenses.
Month Selection: View budgets for any month. Compare current month performance to previous months.
Bills

Track recurring bills and due dates. Mark them as paid when you pay. Unlike Recurring, Bills don't auto-create entries - they're for tracking only.

Bill Cards: Each bill shows name, category, amount, due day, frequency, payment method, paid date, and next due date. Shows urgency color (red if overdue, amber if due soon, green if paid).
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new bills or modify existing ones. Set due day, frequency, and payment method.
Mark Paid Button: Click to mark a bill as paid. Records the payment date and calculates the next due date. Shows confirmation with date.
Button Re-enables: The mark paid button re-enables 7 days before the next due date. Prevents accidental double payments.
Paid Badge: Shows a green "Paid" badge when a bill has been paid. Displays the paid date for reference.
Activate/Deactivate: Toggle bills as active or inactive. Inactive bills don't show in upcoming reminders but remain in history.
Payment Method Linking: Associate bills with specific payment methods for tracking (e.g., credit card, bank account).
Next Due Date: Automatically calculates the next due date based on frequency (monthly/yearly/quarterly). Accounts for leap years and month variations.
Archiving: Inactive bills are archived after 2 years. Keeps your view clean while preserving historical data.
Difference from Recurring: Bills are for tracking only - they don't auto-create expense entries. Use Recurring if you want automatic expense creation.
Subscriptions

Track subscription services like Netflix, Spotify, etc. Shows monthly equivalent for yearly plans to help you compare costs.

Subscription Cards: Each subscription shows service name, amount, billing cycle (monthly/yearly), next billing date, and category. Shows monthly equivalent for yearly plans.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new subscriptions or modify existing ones. Track all your digital subscriptions in one place.
Monthly Equivalent: Yearly plans are converted to monthly equivalent for easier comparison. Helps you understand true monthly cost.
Reminder Only: Subscriptions are for reminder purposes only - they don't auto-debit accounts. You must manually pay subscriptions.
Payment Method Linking: Associate subscriptions with payment methods for tracking. Know which card to use for each subscription.
Next Billing Date: Shows when the next payment is due. Color-coded urgency (red if overdue, amber if due soon).
Total Monthly Cost: Summary card shows total of all subscription costs per month. Helps you understand subscription spending.
Savings Goals

Track progress toward your financial savings goals. Set targets and contribute manually to reach them.

Goal Cards: Each goal shows name, target amount, current amount saved, progress percentage, target date, and notes. Visual progress bar shows completion status.
Pagination: Shows 6 savings goals per page for better performance.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new goals or modify existing ones. Set target amount and target date.
Add Contribution: Modal to add contributions to your savings goals. Record each contribution with date and notes.
Progress Bars: Visual indicators showing how close you are to reaching your goal. Color-coded by completion (green when 100% complete).
Manual Contributions: Contributions are manual - there's no auto-debit from accounts. You decide when and how much to contribute.
Target Date: Set a target date for when you want to achieve your goal. Helps you plan and track progress over time.
Archiving: Goals are archived after 2 years when 100% complete. Keeps your active goals view clean.
Contribution History: View all contributions to a goal with dates and amounts. Track your saving pattern over time.
Accounts

Manage your bank accounts, wallets, cash, and credit cards. Balances update automatically from linked transactions.

Account Cards: Each account shows name, balance, type (Bank, Wallet, Cash, Credit Card), account number, and notes. Credit cards show as negative balances.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new accounts or modify existing ones. Track all your financial accounts in one place.
Auto-Balance Updates: Account balances automatically update when you link expenses, income, or transfers to them. No manual balance updates needed.
Account Types: Bank accounts (savings, current), Wallets (Paytm, PhonePe, GPay, etc.), Cash (physical cash), and Credit Cards.
Credit Card Debt: Credit card balances are shown as liabilities in your Net Worth calculation. Reduces your net worth appropriately.
Account Number: Optional field for storing account numbers for reference. Helps identify accounts with similar names.
Balance Calculation: Starting balance + (income credited) - (expenses debited) + (transfers in) - (transfers out). Always accurate.
Investments

Track your investments in stocks, mutual funds, FDs, gold, etc. Manual entry with returns calculation.

Investment Cards: Each investment shows name, type (Stock, Mutual Fund, FD, Gold, etc.), invested amount, current value, returns percentage, purchase date, and notes.
Pagination: Shows 10 investments per page for better performance.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new investments or modify existing ones. Update current value to track returns.
Manual Entry: Investments are manually entered - there's no live price feed integration. You update values as needed.
Returns Calculation: Returns are calculated as (current value - invested amount) / invested amount × 100. Shows both absolute and percentage returns.
Net Worth Impact: Investments are shown as assets in your Net Worth calculation. Increases your net worth by current value.
Investment Types: Support for various investment types including stocks, mutual funds, fixed deposits, gold, PPF, EPF, real estate, and more.
Performance Tracking: Track how your investments perform over time by updating current values periodically.
Loans

Track your loans with EMI calculations and repayment schedules. Outstanding balances shown as liabilities.

Loan Cards: Each loan shows lender name, principal amount, interest rate, tenure, EMI amount, start date, and outstanding balance. Shows progress toward payoff.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new loans or modify existing ones. Enter loan details to auto-calculate EMI.
EMI Auto-Calculation: EMI is automatically calculated based on principal, interest rate, and tenure using standard EMI formula. No manual calculation needed.
Repayment Schedule: Full repayment schedule showing each EMI due date, principal component, interest component, and remaining balance. View complete amortization schedule.
Mark EMIs Paid: Manually mark EMIs as paid when you make payments. Updates outstanding balance and remaining schedule.
Outstanding Balance: Tracks remaining loan balance after each payment. Shows how much you still owe.
Net Worth Impact: Outstanding loan balances are shown as liabilities in Net Worth. Reduces your net worth appropriately.
Payment Method Linking: Associate loans with payment methods for tracking. Know which account to use for EMI payments.
Payoff Date: Shows when the loan will be fully paid based on the repayment schedule. Helps with long-term planning.
Lendings & Borrowings

Track money you've lent to others or borrowed from others. Auto-creates expense/income entries for accurate accounting.

Lending Cards: Each entry shows person name, amount lent/borrowed, date, and notes. Shows net balance with each person (positive = they owe you, negative = you owe them).
Pagination: Shows 12 lending entries per page for easier navigation.
I Lent: When you lend money, an expense is automatically created and your account is debited. Ensures your balance reflects the lending.
I Borrowed: When you borrow money, an income is automatically created and your account is credited. Reflects the borrowed amount as income.
Settlement: When you settle a lending/borrowing, it reverses the account entry automatically. Adjusts your balance to reflect the settlement.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new entries or modify existing ones. Track all lendings and borrowings in one place.
Detailed History: Click on stat cards or person names to see detailed transaction history with that person. View all transactions chronologically.
Archiving: Fully settled entries are archived after 6 months. Keeps your active view clean while preserving history.
Net Balance Calculation: Automatically calculates net balance with each person. Positive = they owe you money, Negative = you owe them money.
Split Expenses

Split expenses with friends and track who owes whom. Auto-creates Lending entries for settlement.

Split Cards: Each split shows description, total amount, date, participants list, and split type (equal/custom). Shows settlement status for each participant.
Add/Edit/Delete: Create new splits or modify existing ones. Add participants and specify split amounts.
Auto-Creates Lendings: Splits automatically create Lending entries per participant for settlement tracking. No manual entry needed for each person.
Split Types: Equal split (divides total evenly among all participants) or Custom split (specify exact amounts per person).
Settlement: Settle splits via the Lendings section. When you settle, it updates both the split and the lending entry.
Status Tracking: Status shows "Settled" only when all participants have settled their share. Shows "Partially Settled" for in-progress settlements.
Per-Person Breakdown: Shows how much each person owes or is owed. Clear view of individual responsibilities.
Archiving: Fully settled splits are archived after 6 months. Keeps your active splits view clean.
Trip Expenses

Group expenses under trips and split costs with travel companions. Auto-creates Lending entries.

Trip Cards: Each trip shows name, dates, destination, total spent, and participants. Shows per-person breakdown of costs.
Add/Edit/Delete Trips: Create new trips or modify existing ones. Set trip dates, destination, and add participants.
Add Expenses to Trips: Add expenses to trips with description, amount, category, who paid, and split among participants. Each expense can have different participants.
Auto-Creates Lendings: Trip expenses automatically create Lending entries per participant. Settlement handled through Lendings section.
Settlement: Settle trip expenses via the Lendings section. View all trip-related settlements in one place.
Trip Summary: Shows total trip cost and per-person breakdown. Helps you understand who owes what for the entire trip.
Participants: Add multiple participants to each trip for cost splitting. Each participant can be included or excluded from specific expenses.
Expense Categories: Use standard expense categories for trip expenses (transport, accommodation, food, activities, etc.).
Analytics

Visual reports and charts to understand your spending patterns. Filter by time period for focused analysis.

Time Filters: Filter reports by 3 months, 6 months, or 1 year. Helps analyze short-term vs long-term trends.
Daily Spending Profile Heatmap: Visual calendar showing spending patterns across days of the month. Darker colors = higher spending. Identifies high-spending days.
Category Trends Chart: Line chart showing spending trends across categories over time. Compare category spending month-over-month.
Top Categories: Shows top 6 categories by spending amount. Helps identify your biggest expense categories.
Monthly Spending Comparison: Compare spending across different months. Bar chart shows month-by-month spending totals.
